# Student VC Fund Simulation — Features

## 1. Primary user
- **Student club members** learning how venture capital works by running a simulated fund (pitching, evaluating startups, voting on investments, tracking outcomes).

## 2. Single most important thing
- A member can **review a startup pitch and cast a simulated investment decision** that gets recorded against the fund's portfolio.

## 3–5. Feature checklist

- [ ] **Member accounts & roles** *(MVP)* — Lets students sign up and log in as either an Analyst (votes) or Partner (final decision-maker) so the simulation mirrors a real fund's structure.
- [ ] **Startup deal listings** *(MVP)* — A page where pitches (name, sector, ask amount, pitch deck, valuation) are posted for the club to review.
- [ ] **Investment voting & decision flow** *(MVP)* — Each member can score/vote on a deal, and the system records whether the fund "invested" and how much.
- [ ] **Fund portfolio dashboard** — One screen showing the simulated fund's remaining capital, active investments, and total committed so members can see the big picture.
- [ ] **Outcome simulation (exits & failures)** — A way to mark deals as "exited," "still active," or "failed" with a return multiple, so members learn from results over time.
- [ ] **Discussion / memo per deal** — A comment thread or short investment-memo box on each deal so members can debate before voting.
- [ ] **Leaderboard or member stats** — Tracks each member's pick history and hit rate to make the simulation engaging and competitive.
- [ ] **Admin controls** — Lets a club lead add new deals, reset the fund, and manage members each semester.

## Why these 3 as MVP
Accounts + deal listings + voting are the **smallest loop that actually simulates a fund**. Without all three, there's nothing to do. Everything else (portfolio view, outcomes, memos) makes it richer, but the core experience works without them on day one.
